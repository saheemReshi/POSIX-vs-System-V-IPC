Ah, your shell (it looks like you are using Zsh) is stubbornly refusing to accept the new limits. This happens because some shells cache their limits, or a background process like tmux or systemd is immediately resetting them.

Let’s bypass your shell entirely. We will launch a temporary, elevated sub-shell that has the limits "baked in." Once you close it, everything disappears.

Here is the foolproof way to run this without touching any system configurations:

1. Open a temporary shell with the injected limits
Run this command. It will open a new bash session as root, but with the massive limits explicitly forced onto it:

Bash
sudo prlimit --msgqueue=4300000:4300000 --nofile=65536:65536 bash
2. Verify the limits (Inside the new shell)
Your prompt will likely change to root@.... Check that the limits successfully applied:

Bash
ulimit -q
ulimit -n
(You should now see 4300000 and 65536).
